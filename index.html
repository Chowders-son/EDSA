Awareness & Inclusion

The Kingdom of Eswatini is a small, landlocked country in Southern Africa. Bordered by South Africa to the west and Mozambique to the east. it is one of the few remaining absolute monarchies, led by King Mswati III. Despite our size, Eswatini boasts a rich cultural heritage, with traditions such as the Umhlanga (Reed Dance) and Incwala (First Fruits Ceremony), and the nation is committed to improving healthcare, education, and social inclusion for all, including individuals with Down Syndrome.

We are committed in educating the public to foster knowledge, appreciation, and awareness of Down Syndrome and promoting full inclusion of individuals with Down Syndrome in our Swati society. Our intention includes working with government bodies, development partners, and organizations to improve the lives of individuals with Down Syndrome and ensure access to necessary community facilities.

Support & Services

ESDA is a positive contributor in establishing and planning day centers, workshops, special care units, and other facilities for education and treatment. Our hope is to provide access to occupational therapy, physical therapy, technical training and vocational training to ensure the skills and independence of individuals with Down Syndrome are natured. By offering guidance, knowledge, and emotional support for new parents of children with Down Syndrome, and raising funds for individuals struggling to access essential services, we hope to improve the inclusion of all families in our community.

Research & Policy

Contributin to the eduaction surrounding Down Syndrome, we continuously encourage medical, educational, and vocational research to improve care and quality of life. and assist in creating policies and laws that promote the rights and inclusion of individuals with Down Syndrome. Supporting and promoting research efforts related to Down Syndrome for growing improvements and documenting a road to a more supportive Swazi community.
